Topics
T-Shirt Printing Operations and Design Workflow
Creative Divine Concepts Ltd explained their core business of large-scale T-shirt printing and the challenges non-designers face. They emphasized that clients and operators do not need to learn design from scratch but must understand key specifications such as 22 inches by 12 inches print dimensions. The company highlighted that their support model — guiding clients step-by-step through clicks and settings — makes it easier for machine buyers to operate independently. The goal is to enable people without deep design knowledge to produce professional results, with Creative available for support when problems arise.

Photoshop Realistic Embroidery Plugin Demonstration
Creative Divine Concepts Ltd demonstrated a Photoshop plugin called Realistic Embroidery using a Cadbury logo as the example artwork. The process involved two steps: first applying embroidery to the border, then filling the interior of the artwork with the embroidery effect before rendering. The final embroidered artwork was then placed on a T-shirt mockup to show the client how the finished product would look. The presenter also noted that the same artwork could alternatively be taken to DTF (Direct to Film) printing with an embroidery effect rather than actual physical embroidery, giving clients flexible production options.

Mockup Previews as a Sales and Trust-Building Tool
Creating digital mockups before physical production was presented as a critical cost-saving and trust-building strategy. Physical embroidery samples can cost approximately 2,500, making them expensive for both the business and the client. By producing a realistic digital preview first, the company demonstrates professionalism, shows clients a clear direction, and avoids unnecessary sampling costs. This first-contact approach builds client trust from the initial interaction through to the purchase decision, ensuring clients feel confident and informed throughout the process.

Business Model: Machine Sales and Partner Ecosystem
Creative Divine Concepts Ltd outlined their broader business model: selling printing machines to clients who then become ongoing partners. The company's goal is to retain clients long-term by supplying T-shirts, providing design support, and continuously adding value. This partner-based approach is designed to build a strong, loyal client base rather than one-off transactions. The model also extends to selling design knowledge and artwork creation services, not just physical products — citing a pricing example of 1,500 per T-shirt (1,000 for design and 500 for printing), emphasizing that design quality is the most critical factor in the final product outcome.

Expanding the Business into Design Presets and Digital Services
The company is actively researching design tools and techniques used in Western markets to integrate as presets and resources on their website. The planned website will host Photoshop presets, After Effects assets, and Premiere Pro tools, with users paying credits or fees to access them. The broader vision is to expand from purely selling printed products to also monetizing design knowledge and assets — making the business relevant to designers, startups, and printing businesses alike. The presenter noted that this work is labor-intensive but scalable.

Value Proposition for Startups and Knowledge Monetization
Creative Divine Concepts Ltd articulated a clear value proposition for startup clients: by paying a relatively small fee (e.g., 10,000) for knowledge or services, a client can generate significantly larger revenues (e.g., 100,000 or more, repeatedly). The company positions itself as a one-time knowledge provider that enables clients to generate ongoing returns, justifying the service fee. This philosophy underpins both their teaching program and their design consultancy model. They also stressed the importance of remaining affordable and accessible to startups, as the company itself understands startup challenges firsthand.

Online Teaching Program and Course Pricing
Multiple requests (at least six) have been received for teaching services, prompting the team to formalize an online education offering under the Creative brand. The program would cover full-stack development (led by Jack Blessed remotely), design, and marketing — each taught by a dedicated instructor. Jack would teach online while Creative supervises students physically on-site. The teaching portal would be separate from the main design site but remain under the Creative umbrella. Jack was asked to prepare a pricing/plan list for the development teaching module. The model is designed to be affordable for startups while also catering to higher-paying clients (referenced as clients paying up to 2 million per month).

AI Integration and Color Presets Limitation
Jack Blessed raised the idea of integrating AI image generation into the workflow so that users could drop an image and apply creative transformations (e.g., adding rainbows or effects). Creative Divine Concepts Ltd clarified that AI generation cannot be mixed with their current system because AI does not understand their specific color presets. Instead, the workflow relies on downloadable presets that users apply manually. This represents a current technical limitation of the platform.

Client Project Timeline and Pricing Structure
Discussion covered the timeline and fee structure for an upcoming client deal referenced in a previously shared document (referred to as the cloud document). A three-month project duration was confirmed as acceptable by both parties. The pricing model discussed involves either monthly fees (charged fully per month over three months) or a one-time fee at a reduced rate. The strategy agreed upon is to first demonstrate a thorough understanding of the client's problem before presenting pricing, positioning the solution's value ahead of the cost.
